Why Do We Need an Audit?

Across the globe, businesses are subject to various regulatory frameworks that require statutory audits to ensure financial transparency and compliance. Medium and large organizations, including publicly listed companies, are typically mandated by law to undergo annual audits. These audits provide a comprehensive overview of a company’s financial health, assess risks, and confirm adherence to applicable regulations. To maintain credibility and accuracy, statutory audits must be performed by qualified auditors who meet the standards set by their respective jurisdictions.

Globally, a properly conducted statutory audit not only satisfies regulatory requirements but also delivers immense value to stakeholders. It offers directors, shareholders, and other key parties a clear understanding of the company’s financial position, serving as a foundation for strategic planning, funding decisions, and future growth initiatives.
